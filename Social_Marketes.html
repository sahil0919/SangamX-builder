Building societal markets

Our fundamental view is that a full-service free-markets mechanism, with the SSE as the vital backbone, is needed for this Societal Markets free markets mechanism to be a force multiplier for SEs as they have been for commercial businesses. Else, the SSE stands the risk of merely being a highly complex and costly sourcing alternative to SEs.

Non-profit organisations and Social Enterprises: Organisations with credible intent to solve societal problems, and wanting access to quality, long-term capital.

Institutional and individual impact investors who are truly wanting to invest financial resources they have towards betterment of society, or in other words - investors who value societal returns over financial returns.

Specialists: Highly credible, long standing organisations (NPOs, Universities, Think Tanks) who provide an independent on-the ground assessment of intended social impact.

Social Auditors (whose role is now being formally defined by the ICAI) who would audit the impact being articulated by NPOs/ FPSE, with reference to the standards codified by ICAI.

Capacity-building Agencies would assist the budding NPOs to build the robustness needed in their execution approach so that they can compete effectively and succeed.

Enablers and intermediaries with stated and clear intent to improve societal impact. These intermediaries are needed to remove the adoption friction, to ease the flows around the mechanisms, and to evangelize the new industry operating model.

What is a Social stock exchange (SSE)?

A social stock exchange is the first, vital, foundational mechanism needed to bring such a free market mechanism to life.

· SSEs are exchanges on which NPOs and FP-SEs (For Profit Enterprises focused on social impact), can list themselves and issue fund raising instruments such as bonds, equity etc.

· SSEs will aim at unlocking large pools of social capital (for NPOs), and encourage blended finance structures, so that conventional capital can partner with social capital to address the urgent challenges of society

· Listing and access to the SSE is linked to disclosure & audit requirements relating to financials, governance, operational performance and social impact

This Societal Markets that we envision will be built on the Social Stock Exchange (SSE) as the underlying backbone.

Significant progress in India

In India, the Ministry of Finance, and SEBI have made significant progress in operationalizing the SSE

  • Formation of SSE “segments” on BSE & NSE have been notified by SEBI
  • An Advisory Committee (AC) has been formed to guide and govern the evolution of the SSE
  • Cross-industry collaboration and engagement through Working Groups, Technical Group and now the AC
  • Operating framework covering rules around eligibility, audit, disclosure etc. for listing on the SSE have been published
  • Introduced a new instrument called Zero Coupon Zero Principal bond (ZCZP) that can be listed (issued) on the SSE
  • The role of a “Social Auditor Firm” and “Social Auditors” has been introduced to serve as the auditor at the time of listing as well as verification of ongoing disclosures

This level of clarity and support from the policy makers and regulators in India is unique in the world.

Now, building the core of societal markets

In a free market mechanism, the various balancing forces of the market participants are invoked, measured, aligned to achieve the best possible outcome for the investment, by a continuously moving “price”.

This “price” is really an assessment (or valuation) of the organization’s ongoing ability to deliver on its stated objectives.

The price is the pivotal measure that establishes a credible correlation between impact and investment.

  • Without this ticking price, there is no Capital Markets
  • Without this ticking price, there will be no Societal Markets

Ofcourse, we currently cannot create a ticking price for NPOs because the objective of the social impact sector is not (easily measurable) profit, but purpose. And so, we cannot replicate the valuation models that exists in the for-profit sectors to the social impact sectors.

The core of the SangamX proposition is to create that core – an “Impact Ticker” that will enable all the market participants to continuously align to delivering societal impact at scale.
